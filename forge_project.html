Forge V1


Recently I have been working on a propane backyard forge. I am doing this as a hobby, and I wanted to document some of the effort that it took to make this project.

Initial Desing


Propane Burner


The first design I attempted to create was build using a dual venturi burner setup using this as the main resource for my design. My first burner differed in a few ways: I had two burners, which could be run in either one or two burner mode using a valve, and my gas was feed thorugh the top.

Main fuselage


The main fuselage of the forge is made of a propane tank and various pieces of scrap metal. The forge is lined with 2 layers of 2" kaowool, and coated with a refractory cement (refractory cement turned out to be the incorrect material for this job.) The burner ports are made of two 2" scrap pieces of tube stock.

Due to head loss problem I added doors to the front and rear of the forge. This modification drasitaically improved the efficency of my forge; I was not able to get efficency measuments but I suspect the efficency improvment was in the rance of 100%-200%. In addition I added a small platform to the from of the forge for resting objects on.
